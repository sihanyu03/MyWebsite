I am Sihan, a final-year Information & Computer Engineering student at the University
of Cambridge. I'm interested in performance engineering, GPU programming, and the
systems side of ML.

My main focus currently is my master's project: fusela. It is a JIT-compiled library for
batched and fused small-matrix linear algebra on CUDA and Metal. I explored optimisation
strategies for workloads performed on large numbers of small matrices, and built automatic
kernel generation on top.

Alongside my studies I've held leadership roles in several Cambridge societies, including
serving as the President of the Cambridge International Students' Association. 
Outside academics, I swam competitively for Cambridge, and play table tennis.

After graduating this summer, I'm joining a hedge fund as a software engineer.
